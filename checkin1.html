Understanding Cardiometabolic Health Through Research

My Work with the UMass Mind Program

Through my work with the UMass Mind Program, I help lead cardiometabolic screening initiatives focused on prevention and education. By collecting biometric data such as blood pressure, glucose, and lipid levels, I have gained a deeper understanding of how circulation and metabolism interact. This experience has shown me how physiological data becomes meaningful when translated into clear, actionable health guidance for participants.

What Cardiometabolic Research Has Taught Me

  • How blood pressure reflects vascular resistance
  • Why glucose regulation influences long-term cardiovascular risk
  • The importance of preventive health education
  • How small biomarker changes signal broader systemic patterns

Skills I Continue to Develop

  1. Interpreting physiological datasets
  2. Communicating complex health information clearly
  3. Connecting metabolic data to patient outcomes
  4. Applying systems-level thinking to research
